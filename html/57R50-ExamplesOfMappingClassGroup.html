examples of mapping class group


An example of this concept is to take the 2-sphere S2, then one can calculate that

ℳ⁢(S2)=1,

but

ℳ*⁢(S2)=ℤ2.

For the genus one orientable surface, i.e. the torus T=S1×S1, it is known that its (extended) mapping class group

ℳ*⁢(T)=G⁢L2⁢(ℤ),

but usually by the (non-extended) mapping class group, that is, the group of isotopy classes of homeomorphisms that preserve orientations (the Dehn’s twists) is just

ℳ⁢(T)=S⁢L2⁢(ℤ).

In these two examples we see that ℳ* is an extension of ℳ by ℤ2, trivial for the 2-sphere and non trivial for the torus.

For the projective plane ℝ⁢P2 we have

ℳ⁢(ℝ⁢P2)=ℳ*⁢(ℝ⁢P2)=1

And what about the Klein bottle?

ℳ⁢(K)=ℤ2
ℳ*⁢(K)=ℤ2⊕ℤ2

irreducible n-manifold


An n-manifold (http://planetmath.org/TopologicalManifold) M is called irreducible if for each embedding of a standard (n-1)-sphere Sn-1 in M, there is an embedding of a standard n-ball (http://planetmath.org/StandardNBall) Dn in M such that the image of the boundary ∂⁡Dn coincides with the image of Sn-1.

In case of dimension three it can be proved that each irreducible 3-manifold is also a prime (http://planetmath.org/Prime3Manifold) 3-manifold.

immersion


Let X and Y be manifolds, and let f be a mapping f:X→Y. Choose x∈X, and let y=f⁢(x). Recall that d⁢fx:Tx⁢(X)→Ty⁢(Y) is the derivative of f at x, and Tz⁢(Z) is the tangent space of manifold Z at point z.

If d⁢fx is injective, then f is said to be an immersion at x. If f is an immersion at every point, it is called an immersion.

If the image of f is also closed, then f is called a closed immersion.

The notion of closed immersion (http://planetmath.org/ClosedImmersion) for schemes is the analog of this notion in algebraic geometry.

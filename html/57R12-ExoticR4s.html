exotic R4’s


If n≠4 then the smooth manifolds homeomorphic to a given topological n- manifold, M, are parameterized by some discrete algebraic invariant of M. In particular there is a unique smooth manifold homeomorphic to ℝn.


By contrast one may choose uncountably many open sets in ℝ4, which are all homeomorphic to ℝ4, but which are pairwise non-diffeomorphic.


A smooth manifold homeomorphic to ℝ4, but not diffeomorphic to it is called an exotic ℝ4.


Given an exotic ℝ4, E, we have a diffeomorphism E×ℝ→ℝ5. (As there is only one smooth manifold homeomorphic to ℝ5). Hence exotic ℝ4’s may be identified with closed submanifolds of ℝ5. In particular this means the cardinality of the set of exotic ℝ4’s is precisely continuum.


Historically, Donaldson’s theorem led to the discovery of the Donaldson Freedman exotic ℝ4.
